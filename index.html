Research

My research focus is phenomenology beyond the standard model. Phenomenology is the study of particle physics phenomena, the ultimate goal being to understand the fundamental building blocks of nature and how they interact with one another. Over the past century or so physicists have constructed an elegant model that can, in principle, explain most of what we observe. This is known as the Standard Model of particle physics. We know, however, that it is not complete and there are several unsolved puzzles that we need to address. To that end, theorists (such as myself) work hand-in-hand with experimentalists to look for new physics. In other words we look for physics beyond the standard model.

I have previously worked on supersymmetry, dark matter, and their overlap, but most recently I have been more interested in neutrino physics. In particular, I would like to investigate what we could learn from Coherent Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering, or CEvNS (commonly pronounced "Sevens"). I have also collaborated with experimentalists from ATLAS and CMS in sensitivity studies for resonance searches at the LHC.

You can view my publications on INSPIRE.

Music Composition

If you are writing music for someone else to perform, say an ensemble or an orchestra, you have to produce a score, which could be quite laborious and technically involved. But if you are singlehandedly producing the music, as do many indie video game composers, you needn't even a clue how sheet music works. All you need is a capable computer and a DAW (Digital Audio Workstation). The DAW lets you mix and edit audio tracks, and provides a library of samples which you can think of as virtual instruments. With enough experience you can generate stunning results using freely available software. I was inspired to try out composition by a Twitter group called the Music Weeklies Challenge: you are given a theme every week and tasked to compose original music around that theme. I've submitted a few entries in various genres, some of which received rather favorable feedback.

Digital Art
My take on a landscape tutorial by James Julier using ProCreate.

I have always struggled with the mechanics of drawing; my hands are unsteady and my sense of proportions is dreadful. Digital art software drastically mitigate, if not completely bypass, these issues so I can focus on the ideas, on how simple combinations of shapes and colors can be assigned physical interpretations by our brains. With the performance and features of modern software and hardware, and the plethora of online tutorials, art has never been so accesible for the beginner. I'm currently using the ProCreate app on an iPad Pro with the Apple Pencil 2. I alternate between abstract automatic drawings and cartoonish art, and occasionally make an attempt at a detailed landscape tutorial.
